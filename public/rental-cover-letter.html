What makes a good rental cover letter

A good cover letter is short, genuine, and tells the agent three things: who you are, why this property is right for you, and why you'll be a reliable tenant. It's not a CV — it's a one-page introduction that makes the agent feel comfortable handing you the keys.

The biggest mistake renters make is using a generic template that mentions nothing specific about themselves or the property. Agents see through it instantly. RenterIQ's builder asks you a few questions about yourself and the property, then writes a genuinely personalised letter you can edit and submit.

The three things every cover letter must include

  • Who you are — your name, what you do, where you're moving from and why. Keep it brief but warm.
  • Why this property — what specifically appeals to you about this place. Mention the suburb, the layout, the proximity to work or family — anything that shows you're not just spamming applications.
  • Why you're a great tenant — clean rental history, stable employment, references available, respectful of property. Specific is better than generic.

What to avoid

  • Generic openings — "To whom it may concern" is fine but lifeless. "Dear [Agent Name]" is much warmer if you can find their name.
  • Begging — desperation is a red flag for agents. Be confident, not pleading.
  • Mentioning bond / legal threats — this is not the place for tenancy law commentary.
  • Long-winded life stories — keep it under 250 words. Agents skim.
  • Spelling and grammar errors — use a spell-checker. Proofread once.

Cover letter structure (template)

Dear Property Manager,

I'm writing to express my genuine interest in [property address]. I'm [your name], a [profession] currently working at [employer], and I'm looking for a long-term home that I can really settle into.

[Property feature you love] is exactly what I've been looking for. I particularly value [specific aspect — light, layout, proximity to work, garden, etc.], and the location works perfectly for [reason — commute, family, lifestyle].

I take my responsibilities as a tenant seriously. I've been at my current address for [duration] and have always paid rent on time, kept the property clean, and communicated promptly with my agent. I'm a non-smoker, [pet status], and can provide references from my current and previous landlords on request.

Thank you for considering my application. I'd welcome the opportunity to view the property again or answer any questions you may have.

Kind regards,
[Your name]

Frequently asked questions

How long should a rental cover letter be?

200–300 words is the sweet spot. Long enough to introduce yourself genuinely, short enough that a busy agent will actually read it. RenterIQ's builder targets ~250 words by default.

Can I use the same cover letter for multiple properties?

You can use the same structure, but always swap out the property-specific details (address, suburb, what you love about the place). Agents notice when a letter is clearly recycled. RenterIQ's builder makes it easy to generate a fresh letter per property.

Should I include my income?

It's not strictly required in the cover letter — your rental application form will have it. But mentioning that you're employed full-time, the role and the employer adds credibility without giving exact figures.

What if I have pets?

Be upfront. In most Australian states, agents can't unreasonably refuse pets — but you still want them to feel comfortable. Mention your pet briefly, including breed, age and behaviour (e.g. "well-trained, indoor only").
