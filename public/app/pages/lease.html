📅Lease Term
Important
✦ In plain English
Your lease runs for a fixed term. After this date, if neither you nor the landlord gives notice, the lease automatically rolls over to a month-by-month arrangement at the same rent.
Original wordingShow ›
The tenancy commences on [start date] and shall continue for a fixed term expiring on [end date] ('the fixed term') unless sooner terminated in accordance with this agreement.
💰Weekly Rent
Standard
✦ In plain English
Your weekly rent is payable every week on the agreed day. Payment must be made by direct bank transfer to the agency's trust account. Rent is considered late if it is not received by close of business on the due date.
Original wordingShow ›
The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord rent in the agreed amount per week, payable in advance on the agreed day by direct bank transfer to the Agent's nominated trust account.
💰Rent Increases
Important
✦ In plain English
The landlord can increase the rent, but only after giving you 60 days written notice. During a fixed-term lease, rent can only be increased if the agreement specifically allows it and states the new amount or how it will be calculated. In Victoria, rent cannot be increased more than once every 12 months.
Original wordingShow ›
Rent may be increased during the tenancy in accordance with the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) upon provision of not less than sixty (60) days written notice to the Tenant.
🔑Bond / Security Deposit
Important
✦ In plain English
A bond equivalent to 4 weeks rent has been collected. This must be lodged with the Residential Tenancies Bond Authority (RTBA) by the agent within 10 business days. You should receive a bond lodgement receipt — keep this safe in your Vault. The bond can only be claimed for unpaid rent, damage beyond fair wear and tear, or cleaning costs.
Original wordingShow ›
A bond equivalent to four (4) weeks rent shall be paid and held in accordance with the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) and lodged with the Residential Tenancies Bond Authority.
🔧Repairs & Maintenance
Standard
✦ In plain English
The landlord must keep the property in good repair. For urgent repairs — like a burst pipe, gas leak or broken heater — the agent must be contactable 24/7 and must respond promptly. For non-urgent repairs, you must notify the agent in writing. Always report issues in writing (text or email) so you have a record.
Original wordingShow ›
The Landlord shall maintain the premises in good repair. The Tenant must notify the Landlord or Agent of any damage or need for repair as soon as practicable after becoming aware of the damage or need for repair.
🐾Pets
Review
✦ In plain English
Pets are not permitted at this property without the landlord's prior written consent. If you have or plan to get a pet, you must apply in writing before bringing it to the property. In Victoria, landlords cannot unreasonably refuse a pet — but they can impose reasonable conditions.
Original wordingShow ›
No animals or pets of any description shall be kept at the premises without the prior written consent of the Landlord or Agent, such consent not to be unreasonably withheld in accordance with Part 12 of the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic).
⚠️
This is standard wording — however if you have a pet or are considering one, make sure you have written consent before signing. Victoria's rental laws give you the right to request pet approval.

🔨Alterations & Modifications
Review
✦ In plain English
You cannot make any changes to the property — including hanging picture hooks, painting, or installing fixtures — without written permission from the landlord. In Victoria, minor modifications (like picture hooks and furniture anchors) cannot be unreasonably refused. Always get permission in writing.
Original wordingShow ›
The Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions or improvements to the premises without the prior written consent of the Landlord, which consent may be given or withheld at the absolute discretion of the Landlord.
⚠️
The blanket prohibition on 'all modifications' may be broader than Victorian law allows. Under the RTA 1997, tenants have the right to make certain minor modifications. If refused, you can apply to VCAT.

📋Landlord Entry
Standard
✦ In plain English
The landlord or agent can only enter the property with your agreement, or after giving at least 24 hours written notice (except in emergencies). Entry must occur between 8am and 6pm on weekdays, and only between 9am and 6pm on Saturdays — never on Sundays or public holidays without your consent.
Original wordingShow ›
The Landlord or Agent may enter the premises in accordance with sections 85-91 of the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) upon provision of not less than twenty-four (24) hours written notice to the Tenant.
🚪Breaking the Lease
Review
✦ In plain English
If you need to end the lease early, you must give written notice and pay a break lease fee. The fee depends on how much of the lease is remaining — the earlier you leave, the higher the fee. You may also be responsible for rent until a new tenant is found, plus re-letting and advertising costs.
Original wordingShow ›
In the event the Tenant vacates the premises prior to the expiration of the fixed term, the Tenant shall be liable for a break fee and associated costs as prescribed under the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) including but not limited to re-letting fees and advertising costs.
⚠️
Breaking a lease can be expensive. Before signing, make sure you're confident you can commit to the full term. If your circumstances change, contact Consumer Affairs Victoria for advice on your options.

🧹Cleanliness & Condition
Standard
✦ In plain English
You must keep the property reasonably clean and in the same general condition as when you moved in, allowing for fair wear and tear. Fair wear and tear means gradual deterioration from normal everyday use — it does not include damage, stains, or neglect. Your RenterIQ move-in report documents the original condition.
Original wordingShow ›
The Tenant shall keep the premises in a clean and tidy condition and shall not damage or permit damage to the premises beyond that which constitutes fair wear and tear.
🔇Noise & Nuisance
Standard
✦ In plain English
You must not create excessive noise or nuisance that disturbs neighbours or other residents. This is standard and applies to all tenancies. If you receive a noise complaint, respond calmly and keep a record of the communication.
Original wordingShow ›
The Tenant shall not cause or permit any nuisance at the premises or interfere with the reasonable peace, comfort or privacy of neighbours or persons residing in the neighbourhood.
⚡Utilities & Services
Important
✦ In plain English
You are responsible for paying electricity, gas and water usage charges from the date your tenancy starts. Internet and other services are your responsibility to connect. The landlord pays for water supply charges (the fixed charge on your water bill) — you only pay for usage above the minimum threshold.
Original wordingShow ›
The Tenant shall be responsible for all charges for electricity, gas and water consumption relating to the premises from the commencement of the tenancy. The Landlord shall be responsible for any fixed supply charges as required by the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic).
