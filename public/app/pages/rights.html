Your Rights as a Renter
Applies in all Australian states
🔑
Bond Protection
Your bond must be lodged with the state bond authority — not held by the landlord or agent. You are entitled to a lodgement receipt.
🔧
Repairs & Maintenance
Your landlord must maintain the property in good repair. Urgent repairs must be fixed promptly. You can report non-urgent repairs in writing.
📅
Entry Notice Required
Landlords and agents must give written notice before entering — usually 24 hours minimum. Emergency entry is an exception.
💰
Rent Increase Rules
Rent cannot be increased more than once every 12 months in most states. Proper notice must be given in writing before any increase.
🐾
Pets
In most states, landlords cannot unreasonably refuse a pet. You have the right to apply — they must respond in writing.
🔨
Minor Modifications
In most states, landlords cannot unreasonably refuse minor modifications such as picture hooks, furniture anchors and accessibility fittings.
🚪
Eviction Protections
You cannot be evicted without proper notice and valid grounds. The process must follow your state's Residential Tenancies Act exactly.
⚖️
Fair Wear & Tear
Normal deterioration from everyday use cannot be charged to you at exit. Only damage beyond fair wear and tear can be deducted from your bond.

Victoria-specific tenant rights
Rent increases in Victoria are limited to once every 12 months — regardless of lease type.
Renters have the right to keep a pet unless the landlord provides written grounds for refusal.
Minor modifications (picture hooks, furniture anchors, accessibility features) cannot be unreasonably refused.
From 2021, minimum standards apply — heaters, locks, blinds and vermin-proofing are required.
Family violence provisions allow a tenant to end a lease early without penalty in some circumstances.
⚖️
VCAT — Tenancy Disputes
File a tenancy dispute application online or by phone. Hearings are usually within 4–6 weeks.

NSW-specific tenant rights
Rent increases are limited to once every 12 months in NSW with 60 days written notice required.
NSW introduced a 'no grounds' eviction ban for fixed-term leases — landlords must have a valid reason to end your tenancy.
Tenants can make minor alterations with landlord consent — consent cannot be unreasonably withheld.
NSW has a Rental Bond Board that holds bonds separately from landlords and agents.
Domestic violence tenants have special protections including the right to terminate a lease early.
⚖️
NCAT — Apply for a Hearing
Online applications take around 15 minutes. Most hearings are held within 6 weeks.

Queensland-specific tenant rights
The RTA offers a free dispute resolution service before you need to go to QCAT — use this first.
Rent increases require 2 months written notice and can only happen once every 12 months in QLD.
Queensland has specific rules about entry — agents must give 24 hours notice for inspections.
Tenants in QLD can end a fixed-term lease early in domestic violence situations without penalty.
Bond must be lodged with the RTA within 10 days of receipt by the agent.
⚖️
RTA Dispute Resolution — Free Service
Queensland's free conciliation service resolves most disputes without needing to go to tribunal.

Western Australia-specific tenant rights
WA's Residential Tenancies Act 1987 governs all residential tenancies in the state.
Rent increases require 60 days written notice and cannot occur more than once per year.
Bond in WA is lodged with the Bond Administrator within 14 days of receipt.
The Magistrates Court handles most tenancy disputes — the process is simpler and cheaper than other states.
WA has specific provisions for caravan parks and residential parks — different rules apply.
⚖️
WA Magistrates Court — Tenancy Claims
File a minor case claim for tenancy disputes. Most cases are heard within 8 weeks.

South Australia-specific tenant rights
SA requires 60 days notice for rent increases with a minimum 12-month period between increases.
Bond in SA cannot exceed 4 weeks rent for most residential tenancies.
SA has specific rules about water charges — landlords can only charge for water usage above a prescribed amount in some circumstances.
SACAT provides a free conciliation service before formal hearing — use this first in any dispute.
Tenants in SA have the right to install broadband cabling if it does not damage the property.
⚖️
SACAT — Residential Tenancy Applications
Apply online for a tenancy hearing. Most matters are heard within 4–8 weeks.

Tasmania-specific tenant rights
Tasmania introduced significant rental reforms in 2023 — landlords must have valid grounds to end a fixed-term tenancy.
Rent increases require 42 days written notice and are limited to once every 12 months.
Bond in Tasmania is held by the Rental Deposit Authority — not by the landlord or agent.
Tenants can request urgent repairs in writing and if not addressed, may arrange repairs themselves up to a prescribed limit.
CBOS provides a free dispute resolution service — contact them before going to the Magistrates Court.

ACT-specific tenant rights
The ACT has some of the strongest tenant protections in Australia — landlords must have valid grounds to end any tenancy.
Rent increases are limited to once per 12 months with 8 weeks written notice required.
ACT tenants have the right to keep pets unless the landlord obtains an ACAT order preventing it.
Minimum standards for rental properties apply in the ACT including heating, insulation and ventilation.
The ACT has a dedicated tenancy conciliation service — most disputes are resolved without a tribunal hearing.
⚖️
ACAT — Tenancy Dispute Applications
Apply online or by phone. The ACT's conciliation service resolves most disputes before a formal hearing.

The NT has special provisions for short-term and tourist accommodation that differ from standard residential tenancy agreements.
NTCAT provides a relatively fast dispute resolution service — most hearings are within 6 weeks.
