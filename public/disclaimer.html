1. Not Legal Advice

Nothing on the RenterIQ website, within the App, or in any content generated by the App (including inspection reports, lease summaries, bond comparison reports, or renter rights information) constitutes legal advice.

Information about tenancy law, renter rights, and dispute processes is provided for general educational purposes only. Tenancy laws differ between Australian states and territories and change over time. RenterIQ does not guarantee that the information provided reflects the current law in your state or territory.

For advice specific to your situation, consult your state or territory tenancy authority or an independent legal professional.

2. Automated Analysis & Summaries

To help renters understand their documents quickly, RenterIQ uses Google's Gemini language model to produce plain-English summaries of leases, condition reports, and inspection notices. Automatically generated content:

  • May contain errors, omissions, or inaccuracies
  • Reflects only the information and photos you provide
  • Is not reviewed by a qualified legal professional before being returned to you
  • Is not a substitute for a professional condition report prepared by a licensed property manager
  • Should be reviewed carefully before being used in any formal context

The quality and usefulness of any automatically generated report is directly dependent on the quality and completeness of the information you input. We encourage you to be thorough when documenting property condition. Document text submitted for analysis is processed by Google's Gemini API and is not retained by RenterIQ after the summary is returned to you.

3. Inspection Reports

Inspection reports generated by RenterIQ are intended as personal evidence documentation tools. While they are formatted to a professional standard, RenterIQ does not represent or warrant that any report will be accepted as evidence in a tenancy tribunal, court, or dispute resolution process.

Tribunals assess evidence on its merits. A well-documented, timestamped, room-by-room report will generally support your position — but the outcome of any dispute depends on many factors beyond the tool itself.

4. Lease Analysis

The lease review feature produces a plain-English summary of the clauses in your tenancy agreement and flags terms that may warrant attention. This feature:

  • Is not a substitute for having a lawyer review your lease
  • May not identify every clause that is unusual, unfair, or unenforceable in your state
  • Should be used as a starting point for your own review, not as a definitive legal assessment

If you have concerns about specific clauses in your lease, we recommend contacting your state tenancy authority or a tenancy legal service before signing.

Links to government tenancy authorities and other external resources are provided for convenience. RenterIQ does not control those websites and cannot guarantee their content is current or accurate.
