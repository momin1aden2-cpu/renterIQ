Why a rental inspection report is your strongest bond protection

Most Australian renters lose money at the end of their lease over things they didn't cause — pre-existing scuffs, marks, stains, or wear that the agent claims happened during the tenancy. The single best protection is a thorough, timestamped inspection report on the day you move in (and another the day you move out).

Tribunals — VCAT in Victoria, NCAT in NSW, QCAT in Queensland and equivalent bodies in every state — assess bond disputes based on evidence. A well-documented entry condition report with photos is the kind of evidence that wins.

RenterIQ's free rental inspection tool walks you through every room with a guided checklist, captures timestamped photos automatically, and produces a professional PDF you can email to your agent or store in your vault forever.

What renters often miss

Even careful renters skip these — and they're the spots agents scrutinise hardest at exit:

  • Window tracks — dust and grime build up here. Photograph them clean (or noted as dirty) at entry.
  • Behind the toilet — hard to clean later, easy to forget at entry.
  • Range hood filter — agents check this. Document its state on day one.
  • Inside the oven — open it, photograph it. If it's already greasy, you don't want to be charged for it.
  • Skirting boards — scuffs and marks here are common. Capture them.
  • Shower grout — discolouration from previous tenants is normal but easily blamed on you.
  • Carpet edges — corners near doorways and against walls.
  • Light fittings — note any missing covers, bulbs, or fans.

Frequently asked questions

Is the rental inspection app really free?

Yes. RenterIQ is completely free for Australian renters. You can do unlimited inspections, save unlimited reports, and export unlimited PDFs at no cost. There's no trial, no paywall, no hidden fees.

Do I need to install anything?

No. RenterIQ is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — open it in your browser like any website. You can also add it to your home screen for one-tap access, but it's optional.

Will my photos be uploaded somewhere?

By default your photos stay on your device. When you sign in, they sync securely to your private cloud account so you can access them across devices. Only you can see your records.

Can I send the PDF to my real estate agent?

Yes — the export gives you a clean PDF you can email, AirDrop, or print. Agents are required to accept renter-provided condition reports in most Australian states.

What if my agent provides their own condition report?

Use both. Compare your version against the agent's, note any differences in writing, and keep your version as backup. Tribunals weight the more thorough record.
