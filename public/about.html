Our Story

Built by renters,
for every renter.

From Yokine in Perth to Paddington in Sydney — every Australian tenant walks the same path. Inspection in a rush. A paper form on a Saturday morning. And months later, a bond claim you never saw coming.

Most renters have a version of the same story. You find a place that feels right. You sign the lease, do the walk-through with the property manager, tick the boxes on the paper form they hand you, and get on with life. You live there. You pay your rent on time. You look after it.

Then the tenancy ends, and the bond claim arrives.

Carpet replacement. Wall repainting. Cleaning charges. You know a lot of it isn't fair — marks that were there on day one, wear that was already worn in. You remember. But remembering something and proving something are completely different things.

It happened to us right here in Yokine, Perth. The entry report we signed was the agency's version, checked off in fifteen minutes. No photos that mattered, no timestamps, nothing room-by-room. When the bond claim landed, we had nothing that could stand up against it.

We'd seen the same thing happen to friends in Melbourne, flatmates in Brisbane, family in Sydney. Different cities. Same gap. The outcome follows whoever has the better paperwork — and for most renters, that isn't us.

Every Australian renter deserves the tools to protect their bond.

The system is built around whoever has the better evidence. Agencies have professional condition reports, templates, photos, software. Renters have whatever they had time to scribble on a Saturday morning. RenterIQ exists to close that gap — a proper record, in your pocket, for every renter, everywhere in the country.

After losing our own bond dispute, we started digging into how bond claims actually get decided. We talked to renters across every state. We read the tenancy legislation. We sat through tribunal hearings. One thing kept repeating: the outcome follows the evidence trail. A timestamped, itemised record almost always wins. A signed agency form with three ticks almost always loses.

So we built the tool we wished we'd had. Something that walks you through a property room by room, photographs what matters, timestamps it all, and generates a proper record — before you ever hand the entry form back. Something that reads your lease in plain English before you sign it. Something that keeps every receipt, message and maintenance log in one place, so when the bond claim arrives fourteen months later, you have everything.

That tool is RenterIQ. Made in Perth, built for every renter in Australia — whether you're starting a tenancy in Yokine, Kensington, Redcliffe or anywhere in between.
