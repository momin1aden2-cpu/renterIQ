💡 Tips for a successful exit inspection
· The agent will look at exactly what they documented in the original condition report. Focus your cleaning and repairs on those areas.
· Photographing your cleaning is not required but can be helpful — especially for ovens, carpets and bathrooms.
· If you have had professional carpet cleaning, keep the receipt — agencies cannot charge for cleaning that has been professionally done.
· Fair wear and tear cannot be charged to you. Minor scuffs, small nail holes and faded paint after a long tenancy are expected.

Kitchen Splashback Fix Suggested
The Issue:
Kitchen splashback stain appears larger than documented at move-in. Pre-existing stain was 2cm — exit photo shows approximately 5cm.
✦AI-Assisted Fix Suggestion
Not legal advice
Clean the splashback thoroughly with a degreaser or kitchen cleaner (e.g. Exit Mould or Ajax spray). If the stain has grown due to grease or cooking residue, a professional oven/kitchen clean may be required. Many cleaning companies offer kitchen deep cleans for $80–$150. Bathroom Scuff Mark Fix Suggested
The Issue:
Scuff mark on south bathroom wall approximately 8cm in length. Not present in move-in photos or agency report.
✦AI-Assisted Fix Suggestion
Not legal advice
Lightly sand the scuff mark with fine sandpaper (220 grit) then touch up with matching wall paint. Standard rental white is usually Dulux Vivid White or Taubmans Antique White — both available at Bunnings for under $20. Apply one thin coat, allow 2 hours to dry, apply a second coat if needed. Photograph the repaired area before key handover and save to your Vault as evidence of the repair.

💡 When will you get your bond back?
After handing back the keys, the agency has 14 days to lodge a bond claim with the RTBA. If no claim is made, your bond is automatically returned. If a claim is made, you have the right to dispute it. Your RenterIQ reports are your evidence.
